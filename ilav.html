Say Hello to Aman, the First Romanian Modern Artist 

Built in 1868 according to the painter’s design, with the exterior decoration made in collaboration with the sculptor Karl Storck, the house reflects Aman's vision. The painter's presence continues in every corner of the house, naturally materializing in the virtual presence of Aman, who today virtually welcomes his guests thanks to innovative technology. 

Read more about the project

The Theodor Aman Museum, one of the oldest memorial museums in Romania, was opened in 1908 in the painter's residence, the first house studio of an artist in the country.

One of the few residences in Bucharest that have not changed over time, the house preserves the atmosphere of private life from the Belle Epoque period alongside most of the works of the painter Theodor Aman (1831–1891), considered the first Romanian modern artist.

The curious visitor entering this exquisite museum is greeted by Aman himself, a virtual figure created from the most well-known image of the painter. A digital layer with pre-edited video content is superimposed on a live-view video feed where the visitor is also captured. A PIR sensor triggers the digital layer when it detects the visitor's movement.

The application captures images with the two types of content superimposed - the live view and the digital layer. The moment of the capture is synchronized with the interaction, and Aman's character in the pre-edited video content announces that the photo capture is coming. The captured images are delivered via the local network on a viewing medium (Windows PC with multitouch display) in thumbs format. The visitor is directed to search for the photo, share it on Facebook, or print it on the spot for a fee.

Technology: Mixed Reality with a video wall controlled by an application developed to run on Windows PC, video camera for live-view, and presence sensor.

Theodor Aman Museum, 8 C.A. Rosetti St, Bucharest

Having Tea with Madam Suțu 

Visitors of Suțu Palace, an architectural gem in the heart of Bucharest, step into a world where history meets the future and the boundaries of reality are redefined. Here, the intriguing tale of Irina Suțu comes to life in a digital realm. Irina, a luminary in Romanian high society in the XIXth century, is virtually resurrected within the palace's opulent chambers, once again hosting tea parties and cultural soirees.  

Read more about the project

Suțu Palace, a meticulously preserved palace dating back to the XVIIIth century, is a storied architectural treasure in the heart of Bucharest, enchanting visitors with its neoclassical façade and opulent interiors, offering a glimpse into the aristocratic past of Romania. Each meticulously restored room is a masterpiece bearing witness to the palace's significant role in the nation's cultural and political heritage. From its lavish salons to its impressive galleries, Suțu Palace is a living museum, inviting all who enter to embark on a captivating journey through the ages, celebrating the grandeur of a bygone era.

Irina Suțu, a prominent figure in Romanian high society during the XIXth century, breathed life into these splendid halls. Today, her legacy lives on in every meticulously preserved room, each telling a tale of her unwavering commitment. Through innovative technology, Madam Suțu welcomes the visitor with a cup of tea and the invitation to step into the past and engage with the spirit of Bucharest.

A mixed reality solution blends the past and present through a pseudo-holographic display; pre-edited video content magically materializes before the eyes of the visitor, thanks to the motion-sensitive sensors that respond to presence. While exploring this historic landmark and interacting with captivating characters from another time, visitors are encouraged to take "selfies" and share them across social networks.

Technology: Mixed Reality with a translucent pseudo-holographic display, presence sensor, and application developed to run on Windows PC.

Bucharest City Museum, Suțu Palace, 2 Ion C. Brătianu Boulevard, Bucharest

Conversation Across Time and Borders
with the Generation of 1927

An immersive digital platform changes the Romanian Literature Museum visitor experience by engaging with literary giants from the Generation of 1927. This interactive dialogue transcends the boundaries of time, fostering a personal connection with Mircea Eliade, Emil Cioran, and Eugen Ionesco's work and persona, not as static historical figures but as dynamic, responsive entities.  

Read more about the project

The museum visitors can now step into Romanian literary history through a captivating, non-immersive interactive video display and delve into the narrative of the Generation of 1927, a pivotal period in Romanian literature, culture, and society. This innovative application invites the curious to explore the lives and works of the brilliant writers, poets, and thinkers who shaped this era.

The Romanian Literature Museum's interactive video display is not just a window into the past; it's a dynamic platform for education, inspiration, and cultural enrichment, providing an engaging and informative experience for visitors of all ages. A blend of multimedia content and user-friendly interfaces unearth the intellectual ferment of Mircea Eliade, Emil Cioran, and Eugen Ionesco, their struggles, ideals, and their impact on Romania's cultural landscape. Through this immersive platform, visitors can pose questions, seek insights, and gain a deeper understanding of three iconic figures and their profound contributions to literature, philosophy, and theater.

The application integrates and interprets electronic signals from touch sensors, providing an engaging and responsive user experience. Users can trigger the application to play predefined video files tailored to each touch command by simply touching the display. This interactive feature adds an exciting dimension to the exploration of the Generation of 1927 at the Romanian Literature Museum and ensures a personalized and immersive journey through this era's literary and cultural treasures.

Technology: video display embedded in furniture, Windows PC, touch sensor

Romanian Literature Museum, 8 Nicolae Crețulescu St, Bucharest
